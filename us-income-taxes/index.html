Filing status

A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement account that lets you invest pre- or post-tax dollars with tax advantages. Money grows tax-free while it's in the account.

For 2025, the IRS elective deferral limit used on the slider below is $23,500.

The core tradeoff between the two types

Traditional (pre-tax): You contribute before taxes, lowering your taxable income now. You pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement. Better if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket later.

Roth (post-tax): You contribute after taxes, so no upfront benefit. But withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free. Better if you expect to be in a higher bracket later — or just want tax-free income in retirement.
